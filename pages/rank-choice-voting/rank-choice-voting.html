What is Rank Choice Voting?

A fairer way to vote that gives you more power and more choices

How It Works

1

Rank Your Choices

Instead of picking just one candidate, you rank them in order of preference: 1st choice, 2nd choice, 3rd choice, and so on.

2

First Choices Are Counted

All the first-choice votes are counted. If a candidate has more than 50% of the votes, they win!

3

Elimination Rounds

If no one has 50%, the candidate with the fewest votes is eliminated. Their voters' second choices are then counted.

4

Repeat Until a Winner

This process continues until one candidate has a majority. Your vote always counts toward your highest-ranked candidate still in the race.

Why Rank Choice Voting?

  • No More "Spoiler" Effect: Vote for your favorite without worrying about "wasting" your vote
  • More Civil Campaigns: Candidates seek to be voters' second choice too, reducing negative campaigning
  • Better Representation: Winners must appeal to a broader coalition of voters
  • Higher Voter Turnout: More meaningful choices encourage more participation
  • Saves Money: No need for separate runoff elections
