üìä Data Sources

All market data is based on real-world metrics from trusted sources. Our charts track actual progress and public opinion:

  • Universal Healthcare (UHC): Tracks public support for Medicare for All using data from Kaiser Family Foundation Health Reform polling. KFF conducts regular national surveys asking Americans about their support for a national health plan (Medicare for All). Recent polls show approximately 60-70% support depending on question framing.
  • Ranked Choice Voting (RCV): Tracks the number of jurisdictions that have adopted RCV using FairVote's comprehensive database. As of 2024, over 100 jurisdictions including Maine, Alaska, and numerous cities use RCV for elections. FairVote maintains the most authoritative list of RCV implementations.
  • Campaign Finance Reform (CFR): Tracks states with public campaign financing programs using data from the NCSL Public Financing Database. Currently 19 states plus several municipalities offer some form of public financing for campaigns, from matching funds to full public financing options.
  • Supreme Court Reform (SCR): Tracks public support for Supreme Court reforms (including term limits and expansion) using polling from Pew Research Center and Gallup Supreme Court polls. Recent surveys show around 65-70% of Americans support term limits for Supreme Court justices.
  • Green New Deal (GND): Tracks renewable energy as a percentage of U.S. electricity generation using U.S. Energy Information Administration data. The EIA publishes monthly reports showing renewables (wind, solar, hydro, etc.) now account for over 20% of U.S. electricity generation and growing steadily.
  • Voting Rights Act (VRA): Tracks states with automatic voter registration using NCSL's Automatic Voter Registration tracking. As of 2024, 25 states plus D.C. have implemented some form of automatic voter registration, where eligible citizens are automatically registered when interacting with DMVs or other state agencies.

Note: While stock prices in this simulator fluctuate randomly for gameplay purposes, the historical trend data shown in charts reflects real-world metrics from these authoritative sources. This helps you understand the actual progress of each democracy reform movement.
