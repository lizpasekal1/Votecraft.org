Why Ranked Choice Voting?

Understanding the problem with Winner-Take-All elections

💥 Winner-Take-All is Toxic 💥

Here's what's wrong with our current voting system...

💔

It elects someone most don't want, simply because similar awesome candidates split votes. 😞

📈

Winners represent the lowest-minority in a two party system. 🤬

😶

The system discourages leaders from raising new ideas or challenging their own party, out of fear they'll split/divide support. 😬

☢️

This fuels the polarized negativity we see today. 😡🤬😤

⭐ RCV is Stellar ⭐

Here's why Ranked Choice Voting fixes these problems...

📦

If your first choice can't win, your vote isn't trashed—it moves to your next preference. 🎉

👥

Elections reflect who most actually prefer, not who squeaked by with the lowest-minority. 🤩

💡

The system encourages leaders to raise new ideas without fear of splitting the vote. 🌟

🤝

It rewards consensus instead of division, which is a fairer more collaborative outcome. ❤️

⚖️ The Difference ⚖️

See how these systems compare...

👎
Winner-Take-All
  • 💔 Vote splitting elects unpopular winners
  • 🗑️ Your vote is wasted if your candidate loses
  • 🤬 Encourages negative campaigning
  • 🔒 Forces two-party dominance
  • 👤 Winners can win with tiny minorities
👍
Ranked Choice Voting
  • 🎉 Similar candidates don't split the vote
  • 🗳️ Your vote always counts for someone
  • 🤝 Rewards coalition-building
  • 🎤 More voices, more choices
  • 👥 Winners need broad support
🎗️✨

🌟 A Better Way to Vote 🌟

With Ranked Choice Voting, elections become about finding the candidate most people can support—not just the one who benefits from a divided opposition. 🏆

It's simple: rank your choices 1️⃣2️⃣3️⃣. If your first pick can't win, your vote goes to your next choice. Your voice is always heard! 📣

That's how we build a democracy that works for everyone. ❤️🤝🌎
